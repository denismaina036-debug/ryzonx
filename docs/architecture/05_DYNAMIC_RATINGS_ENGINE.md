# 1. Purpose

The Dynamic Ratings Engine defines how RyvonX evaluates the long-term professionalism, trustworthiness, consistency, transparency, and governance standing of every Pool Manager.

The objective is to provide investors with meaningful indicators that reflect verified platform activity rather than subjective opinions or short-term performance.

Ratings should encourage responsible behavior, transparency, and long-term professional excellence.

---

# 2. Philosophy

Ratings are not marketing badges.

Ratings are not popularity scores.

Ratings are not based solely on profitability.

Ratings exist to help investors understand how a Pool Manager behaves over time.

The engine rewards:

Professionalism

Consistency

Transparency

Capital Preservation

Risk Management

Communication

Governance Compliance

Investor Confidence

Long-term Performance

---

# 3. Core Principles

The Ratings Engine must satisfy the following principles.

Ratings must evolve continuously.

Ratings must be evidence-based.

Ratings must remain explainable.

Ratings must use verified platform data.

Ratings should discourage manipulation.

Ratings should reward long-term consistency over isolated success.

Ratings should be reproducible.

---

# 4. Rating Lifecycle

Every Pool Manager progresses through the following rating lifecycle.

Application

↓

Evaluation

↓

Administrative Assessment

↓

Initial Ratings

↓

Professional Activity

↓

Continuous Rating Updates

↓

Governance Adjustments

↓

Long-Term Reputation

Ratings are intended to evolve throughout the lifetime of the Pool Manager.

---

# 5. Initial Ratings

Upon approval, RyvonX administrators assign baseline ratings.

These represent the initial professional assessment based on:

Application

Evaluation

Trading Journal

Professional Experience

Strategy Review

Risk Management

Transparency

Communication

The initial ratings establish the starting point for future evaluation.

---

# 6. Continuous Rating Evolution

After approval, ratings should evolve automatically using verified platform activity.

Examples of influencing factors include:

Completed Investment Cycles

Trading Performance

Consistency

Risk Control

Journal Completion

Journal Timeliness

Governance Events

Investor Reviews

Communication

Historical Stability

Platform Participation

Ratings should not remain static.

---

# 7. Trust Score

The Trust Score represents the overall confidence RyvonX has in a Pool Manager.

It should summarize multiple dimensions of professional behavior.

Examples of contributing factors include:

Governance History

Transparency

Consistency

Capital Preservation

Historical Performance

Investor Satisfaction

Communication

Professional Conduct

Trust should increase gradually through sustained positive activity.

Major governance actions may reduce Trust where appropriate.

---

# 8. Winning Rate

Winning Rate measures historical trading success.

Winning Rate should derive from verified historical trading data.

The metric should not be editable.

Winning Rate should be presented with appropriate context and should not be treated as the sole indicator of professional quality.

---

# 9. Consistency

Consistency measures stability over time.

Examples of contributing factors include:

Regular positive performance

Controlled drawdowns

Predictable risk management

Sustained Investment Cycle completion

Long-term stability should be rewarded more than short periods of exceptional returns.

---

# 10. Transparency

Transparency reflects how openly a Pool Manager operates within the RyvonX ecosystem.

Examples include:

Complete Trading Journals

Timely Updates

Clear Communication

Investment Cycle Reporting

Accurate Information

Transparency should encourage complete and timely disclosure.

---

# 11. Risk Management

Risk Management evaluates how responsibly capital is managed.

Examples include:

Drawdown Control

Position Sizing

Capital Preservation

Risk Consistency

Strategy Discipline

Risk Management should reward responsible investment behavior rather than aggressive speculation.

---

# 12. Capital Preservation

Capital Preservation reflects the Pool Manager's ability to protect investor capital.

Historical preservation of capital should positively influence long-term reputation.

---

# 13. Governance

Governance reflects compliance with RyvonX standards.

Examples include:

Warnings

Suspensions

Compliance

Professional Conduct

Administrative Reviews

Governance should remain under administrative authority.

---

# 14. Aggressiveness

Aggressiveness describes trading behavior.

It should communicate investment style rather than imply quality.

Examples may include:

Conservative

Balanced

Moderate

Growth

Aggressive

This metric helps investors select strategies aligned with their preferences.

---

# 15. Professionalism

Professionalism evaluates behavior beyond investment performance.

Examples include:

Communication

Platform Participation

Responsiveness

Journal Quality

Compliance

Investor Relations

Professionalism encourages long-term platform quality.

---

# 16. Investor Satisfaction

Investor Satisfaction reflects verified investor experience.

Examples may include:

Reviews

Retention

Repeat Participation

Professional Communication

Issue Resolution

Investor Satisfaction should complement, rather than replace, objective performance metrics.

---

# 17. Governance Influence

Governance events may influence ratings.

Examples include:

Warnings

Compliance Actions

Suspensions

Verification Changes

Administrative Reviews

Every governance adjustment should remain auditable.

---

# 18. Historical Stability

Ratings should value long-term stability.

A manager with years of consistent professional conduct should generally receive greater confidence than one with only short-term exceptional performance.

---

# 19. Administrative Authority

Administrators retain authority to:

Assign Initial Ratings

Issue Governance Adjustments

Apply Manual Rating Corrections

Recognize Exceptional Performance

Address Fraud

Correct Data Errors

Every administrative action should generate an audit record.

---

# 20. Rating Integrity

Pool Managers may never directly edit ratings.

Ratings should originate from:

Verified platform activity

Administrative governance

Historical records

Backend services

Ratings should never rely on frontend calculations.

---

# 21. Rating Visibility

Public ratings should help investors make informed decisions.

Internal calculation details may remain administrative.

Public presentation should remain understandable.

---

# 22. Future Expansion

Future rating categories may include:

Strategy Maturity

Institutional Readiness

AI Confidence Metrics

Regional Compliance

Professional Certifications

Platform Contributions

The Ratings Engine should remain extensible.

---

# 23. Source of Truth

This document defines the official Ratings Engine for RyvonX.

All dashboards, Marketplace pages, Pool Manager Profiles, APIs, analytics, and administrative tools should consume ratings generated through this architecture.

Independent rating implementations are prohibited.
